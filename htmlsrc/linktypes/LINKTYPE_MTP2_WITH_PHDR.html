LINKTYPE_MTP2_WITH_PHDR

Packet structure

+--------------------------------+
|           Direction            |
|           (1 Octet)            |
+--------------------------------+
| Extended Sequence Numbers used |
|           (1 Octet)            |
+--------------------------------+
|           Link number          |
|           (2 Octets)           |
+--------------------------------+
|           MTP2 frame           |
.                                .
.                                .
.                                .

Description

The Direction field is 0 if the packet is going over the link in one direction and 1 if it's going over the link in the other direction. If the capture is being done by the device sending and receiving the packets, 0 should be used for packets received by the device and 1 should be used for packets sent by the device.

The Extended Sequence Numbers used field is 0 if extended sequence numbers, as specified by Annex A of ITU-T Recommendation Q.703, are not being used, 1 if extended sequence numbers are being used, and 2 if it is unknown whether extended sequence numbers are being used.

The Link number field is in big-endian byte order, and indicates the number of the signaling link.

The MTP2 frame is a Signaling System No. 7 Message Transfer Part Level 2 (MTP2) packet, as specified by section 2.2 "Signal unit format" of ITU-T Recommendation Q.703 (note that the fields in Figure 3 are transmitted in right-to-left order). The packet does not include the check bits. The packet is not bit-stuffed, so it includes no flag fields.
